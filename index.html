Title of First Image

The settlement of Ntopwa in Malawi, Africa, is one of the many communities in the world that faces water insecurity. For the people of Ntopwa, there are many risks that arise due to the lack of access to clean water. Women in this community are often the ones tasked with traveling long distances in order to bring back water for their families. They’re are many dangerous risks extended to environmental, physical, and social factors. Some environmental factors include the risk of slipping on terrain, falling, drowning, injury on sharp objects, mosquitos, and road-crossing. Beyond that, water can be contaminated, leading to disease and an increased mortality rate. One of the biggest social downfalls aspects for women in the Ntopwa community is the missed education due to having to fetch water for their families.

How The Lack of Freshwater Has AFfected Vietnam

Lack of freshwater has proved to be a major problem for people living in marginalized communities in Vietnam. The shocking thing I discovered is the articles I read had very little mention on malnourishment and dehydration, but a heavy emphasis on the mental health effects regarding the lack of freshwater. The article stated that the amount of water at disposal is associated with mental health levels. It also concluded the amount of freshwater readily available is determined by socioeconomic status and gave the following as evidence; 94% of urban and 98% of wealthy communities have access to freshwater whereas 78% of rural communities have access, 61% of ethnic minority enhabited areas have freshwater, and 41% of less fortunate areas didn't have access. Those numbers may not seem like a lot, but in comparison 97.33% of Americans have access to readily available freshwater. These people are put at a staggering disadvantage that not only causes malnourishment and dehydration, but causes severe anxiety and depression.
